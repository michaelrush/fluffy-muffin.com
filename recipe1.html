Gingerbread - January 4, 2012

If you want to impress your friends:

You're gonna start off with 3 separate bowls to mix ingredients in, 1. for the butter-sugar mixture, 2. for the liquids ingredients, and 3. for the dry ingredients.

BOWL 1:
0.5 cup of Butter/Margarine
0.5 cup of Sugar
1 egg

The ingredients in this bowl should be mixed particularly well, you want to make sure they're as even a consistency as possible. It's a bit more difficult to get the mixture totally smooth without an electronic mixer, but with a fork and some arm/wrist strength it should end up something like this, with the chunks of butter being extremely small, and the sugar and egg mixed evenly, and you'll be all set to move on.

Bowl 2:
2.5 cups flour
1.5 tsp baking soda
2 tsp cinnamon
2 tsp ginger
1 tsp cloves
0.5 tsp nutmeg
0.5 tsp salt

This mix is simple enough with just a fork, just stir until the powder is one solid light brown color, with no streaks obviously separating any of the ingredients, going from this:

To This:

Next!

Bowl 3:
0.5 cup Molasses
0.5 cup Honey
1 cup hot water

This mixture will start off with the water pretty obviously separated from the other ingredients, like so:

but just stir it with a spoon and periodically take it out to see if the molasses and honey have become less viscous and sticky from mixing with the water, and the mix should become a more solid dark color.

Now to bring it all together!

Now mix the dry and liquid ingredients into the egg/sugar/butter bowl, by basically adding 1/3rd-ish of each bowl to get this:

Then mixing until it's like this:

Then repeating until everything's in one bowl, looking like that.

Home stretch!

Take a 9 x 9 x 2 inch pan (give or take, the pan I used was 8 x 8), make sure to grease/butter/margarine it so that you can actually get the gingerbread out of the pan when it's done!

Sprinkling a bit of flour after the pan is greased will usually help too.

Bake for about 50 minutes, then if the gingerbread seems to have sufficiently swelled inside the pan, stick a toothpick inside to make sure it isn't especially sticky/viscous anymore. If the toothpick comes out clean, take it out to cool and it'll be ready to serve!

Or if you're feeling a bit too lazy for that:

Hop down to your neighborhood Trader Joe's and grab some "Deep, Dark Gingerbread Cake and Baking Mix"! (I'm sure there are plenty of other grocery stores that carry gingerbread mix, but this one was nearby, affordable and tasty, which are usually the three things I look for in quick food, and you should too!)

All you'll need is (as the box says):
The box of mix itself,
One egg
.75 cup of water
1/3 cup of vegetable oil (though we used melted butter and it came out just fine)

As usual, start with preheating the oven! 375 derees this time.

Luckily, this version of the recipe only requires one bowl! Mix the egg, water and oil/butter until they're an even consistency:

Then gradually add the mix from the box, stirring as you go:

And you'll end up with this!

An 8 x 8 pan is recommended, but we used two skinnier pans to make two loaves, so really just use whatever the batter will fit in! Remember, as always, to grease/butter the pans so you can get at that sweet gingerbread out in whole, non-crumbly pieces!
